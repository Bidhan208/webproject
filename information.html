Things to do

Explore Lumbini Museum

The Lumbini Museum is a treasure trove of historical and cultural artifacts which provides deep insight into the life of Gautama Buddha

Lumbini Monasteries Zone

The Lumbini Monastic Zone is one of the most fascinating parts of Lumbini where ountries from around the world have built stunning Buddhist monasteries to represent their culture, architecture, and interpretation of Buddha's teachings.

Meditation session

Lumbini is a living sanctuary of peace, attracting spiritual seekers from all over the world. Many visitors come here not only to explore but also to experience inner peace through meditation.

Festivals and Events

Buddha jayanti

Buddha Jayanti is a significant festival honoring Gautam Buddha’s birth, enlightenment, and passing. In Lumbini, devotees gather for prayers, meditation, and offerings at Maya Devi Temple.

Lhosar

Losar is the Tibetan New Year, celebrated with prayers, rituals, and cultural performances. In Lumbini, devotees mark the occasion with offerings, traditional dances, and festive gatherings at monasteries.

spritual Gathering

Lumbini hosts spiritual gatherings where pilgrims meditate, pray, and seek wisdom at sacred sites. Monasteries offer retreats, teachings, and cultural ceremonies.

Nearby Attractions

Tilaurakot

27 km from Lumbini

Believed to be the ancient capital of Kapilvastu where Prince Siddhartha spent his early life.

Ramgram Stupa

50 km from Lumbini

The only stupa containing Buddha’s relics that has not been opened or disturbed.

Devadaha

35 km from Lumbini

The maternal home of Queen Mayadevi (Buddha’s mother), rich in history and archaeological ruins.
